## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL (to your own method document)

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When a bet’s edge is driven almost entirely by market mispricing and the projected win probability is extremely close to 50% (say 48–52%), add a secondary filter requiring a minimum absolute edge of 6 points (instead of 4) or raising the unit cap for such bets.  

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:**  
The MIN bet possessed an 8.4‑point edge, but the projected win probability was only 50.1% — a virtual coin flip. The edge arose from the line, not from the model’s conviction that one side was superior. In such cases, variance can easily overwhelm the edge, and the bet may not offer sufficient calibrated value relative to the risk of a close loss. Tightening requirements here would reduce noise without systematically filtering out strong projections.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (cite pre‑game data, not the outcome):**  
Pre‑game run projection: LAD 5.40, MIN 5.41 → MIN win% 50.1%. The fair moneyline for MIN was roughly +100, and the available +134 provided an 8.4‑point edge. However, the projected gap in win probability was negligible, meaning the bet’s success depended entirely on the line being wrong by enough over a large sample; on any single day, a coin flip can lose without any flaw in the process. The method currently treats a 4‑point edge uniformly across all win‑probability ranges, which may overvalue near‑50% projections.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM
