## S6: MODEL-SPECIFIC REASONING INSTRUCTION

**MODEL: DeepSeek (deepseek-v4-pro)**
**REASONING WEAKNESS IDENTIFIED: The model overestimates win probability edges when one component (starting pitcher, offensive split) comes from an extremely small sample or a player returning from injury. The edge is taken at face value without an explicit uncertainty adjustment.**
**PROPOSED PROMPT INSTRUCTION:**
“When any single driver of your projected edge relies on a sample of fewer than 30 innings pitched (SP) or fewer than 60 team plate appearances (split), or involves a pitcher coming off an IL stint with no fully stretched‑out start, apply an automatic 5‑percentage‑point haircut to your model’s projected win probability (toward 50%) before comparing to the market. If the edge still meets the threshold, explicitly note the adjustment and its justification in your reasoning.”
**WHY THIS ADDRESSES THE WEAKNESS: It forces a margin of safety for the most volatile, small‑sample elements of a projection. The deGrom edge would shrink from 14.7 to ~9.7, still bettable but more honest. ## S6: MODEL-SPECIFIC REASONING INSTRUCTION

**MODEL:** Claude Sonnet
**REASONING WEAKNESS IDENTIFIED:** When a data flag is encountered mid-reasoning (e.g., "no data" on L14, stale price, data anomaly), the model correctly identifies the flag but then constructs a rationale for proceeding rather than treating the flag as a hard decision node. This leads to bets being made on incomplete information with insufficient discounting.

**PROPOSED PROMPT INSTRUCTION:**

> "When any data integrity flag is encountered during analysis — including 'no data' on L14, stale/suspect market prices, or anomalous metrics (e.g., 19.16 ERA) — treat the flag as a **hard decision node**, not a speed bump. Stop at the flag and explicitly ask: *'What is the most likely explanation for this gap, and does any plausible explanation make this bet wrong?'* If the answer is yes under any plausible explanation, either pass or reduce stake. Do NOT construct a rationale for proceeding past a flag using data from a different time window (e.g., substituting AGG for missing L14). If you substitute, explicitly label the bet: PROCEEDING WITHOUT RECENT FORM CONFIRMATION and cap at 0.5 units regardless of calculated edge."

**WHY THIS ADDRESSES THE WEAKNESS:** The DET ML loss was caused precisely by acknowledging the "no data" flag on Skubal's L14 and then substituting AGG metrics as if they were equivalent. They are not equivalent — a pitcher with no recent L14 data may be returning from IL, on a modified schedule, or dealing with a hidden health issue. The instruction creates a forced pause at every flag rather than allowing motivated reasoning to continue.

**SOURCE OF THIS INSIGHT:** DET @ CLE loss (Skubal L14 "no data" rationalized past); AZ @ CIN loss (Lowder 3 IP L14 sample rationalized as sufficient to anchor a -133 bet)

**EXPECTED IMPACT:** HIGH
