## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

PROPOSED CHANGE: When my own SIERA/K-BB% read identifies a listed favorite's starter as functionally non-viable (K-BB% below ~5% over a meaningful recent window), do not auto-defer to the market with "price reflects it" on heavy favorites (-180 or higher). Flag it as a candidate dog-side or pass, but force an explicit estimate of whether the market has actually priced the arm or just the team name.

WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: I twice (BAL/LAD Young, and the Rodríguez logic) used "the price already reflects all of it" as a pass justification on heavy favorites where I had simultaneously documented a disqualifying starter profile. That phrase is doing too much work — it lets me wave off my own strongest signal. The projection-vs-perception engine should fire just as hard on a mispriced favorite as on a dog.

EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data only): On BAL/LAD I wrote that Young hides "an ugly 4.87 SIERA and 2.0% K-BB% L14" — a clear non-viable-starter signal — then passed on the grounds the -215 price was efficient. I never quantified what % the bad arm justified for the dog. That is an internal inconsistency in process, not a result-based critique.

CONFIDENCE: LOW (one slate; the 2-2 record on sides and the high-favorite price caution both argue for restraint before changing anything)
