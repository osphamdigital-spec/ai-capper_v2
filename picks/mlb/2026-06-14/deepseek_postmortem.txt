### S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL
**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When evaluating bullpen strength for a bet, use **xFIP (or FIP) as the primary metric**, not ERA. If only ERA is available and it deviates extremely from league median, flag the game and manually estimate regression. Cap any bet that depends on an extreme bullpen ERA to 1 unit maximum unless confirmed by underlying peripherals.  
**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** I treated ATL’s 1.52 bullpen ERA as an established elite unit, ignoring that ERA over a small sample can be wildly misleading. Using xFIP would have shown a probable regression, lowering the edge and preventing a max‑unit bet on a falsely clean game.  
**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY:** Pre‑game ATL bullpen ERA (1.52) was cited as a key edge; no mention of any peripherals. The 8‑run blow‑up was a single‑game outcome, but the process failed to account for the inherent noise in that 1.52 number. No pre‑game data could predict the specific result, but recognizing the stat’s unsustainability would have made the edge look smaller and the stakes lower.  
**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — one day isn’t proof, but the principle of regressing extreme reliever ERAs is sound and aligns with broader quantitative practice.
