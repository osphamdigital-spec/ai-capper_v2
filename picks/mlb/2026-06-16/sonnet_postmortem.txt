## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSALS

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** Apply a secondary L14 weighting check specifically for starters with a >1.50 xFIP divergence between AGG and L14 (in either direction), even when the L14 sample falls in the [sm] category (11-19IP). When divergence exceeds 1.50 xFIP points and the sample clears 11IP, flag as "directional override candidate" and increase L14 weight from 40% to 50% for that specific calculation, while still noting the elevated uncertainty.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** The current weighting table applies 40% L14 / 60% AGG for the 11-19IP bracket uniformly. Matthews today had a 13IP sample showing xFIP 5.72 vs. AGG xFIP 4.02 — a +1.70 divergence. Under current method, blended = ~4.70. Under the proposed 50/50 at 1.50+ divergence: 0.50×4.02 + 0.50×5.72 = **4.87**. The higher blended estimate would more aggressively favor MIN ML and may have elevated the edge to bet territory. The Matthews case is the clearest example on this slate, but this situation recurs whenever a starter is genuinely in mid-season decline.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data only):** Matthews L14 xFIP 5.72 / SIERA 5.79 vs. AGG xFIP 4.02 — divergence of +1.70. This was pre-game-available data. The current method acknowledged the divergence as "concerning" but did not fully translate it into a bet decision because the blended number at 40% L14 didn't clearly separate from Rocker's blended estimate. A 50% L14 weight at 1.50+ divergence would create a sharper signal. Separately, Kelly's L14 at 11.0IP (exactly at the [sm] boundary) showed xFIP 5.98 vs. AGG 4.21 — a +1.77 divergence. This did correctly translate to the LAA bet under current method (the blended ~4.92 was already sharp), suggesting the 40% weight may slightly underweight severe deterioration signals even when it produces a correct bet.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — single-slate evidence. Requires validation across 10+ slates before adoption. The risk of over-fitting to recent results (Matthews' bad game) is real. The proposal is structurally sound (larger divergence = higher signal weight) but the 1.50 xFIP threshold and 50/50 secondary weight are illustrative, not finalized. Flag for review after 15 additional slates.
