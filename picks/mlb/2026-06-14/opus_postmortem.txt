## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

PROPOSED CHANGE: When my edge on an ML bet rests primarily on bullpen + lineup (rather than a clear starting-pitcher talent gap), and the starter matchup is "roughly even," cap that bet at a lower priority tier and require either plus-money or a >6-pt edge before staking.

WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: Two of my three bets (HOU especially, SEA secondarily) leaned on relief/lineup edges with near-even or thin starting-pitcher gaps. My one clean win-by-thesis (NYY) had a decisive starter edge AND corroborating factors. The pattern worth watching: bets anchored on a true SP talent gap may be more robust than bets anchored on bullpen-and-offense, because the latter require my projected offense to actually show up — a higher-variance dependency.

EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data, not outcome): NYY bet = 8-pt edge with clear SP gap → my conviction structure was strongest there. HOU bet = explicitly described "roughly even arms," edge built on pen/lineup → weakest justification. SEA = clear SP edge but only 4.8-pt margin against -144 (thin). The bet whose justification leaned least on the starter and most on offense-showing-up is the one I'd most want to re-examine.

CONFIDENCE: LOW (one slate; two losses can both be pure variance — do not over-fit).
