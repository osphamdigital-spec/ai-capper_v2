## SECTION 6 — MODEL-SPECIFIC REASONING INSTRUCTION

MODEL: Claude Sonnet 4.6

REASONING WEAKNESS IDENTIFIED: When a team's ML is heavily favored and the run line sits near even money, I treat the gap between ML-implied win probability (~70%) and run-line implied probability (~49%) as a market inefficiency — but these prices answer two different questions (does this team win vs does this team win by 2+), and the gap may be correctly priced for both outcomes simultaneously.

PROPOSED PROMPT INSTRUCTION:
"When using a ML-to-run-line price gap as evidence of edge, you must independently estimate the probability that the favored team wins by 2 or more runs — do not use the ML win probability as a proxy for this. State both estimates explicitly: your ML win probability estimate, and separately your 2+ run margin probability estimate. A team at -250 on the ML (implied ~71% to win) is typically only 48–55% to cover -1.5 in a low-total game environment — these are internally consistent prices, not a mispricing. Only confirm the run line as a qualifying bet if your independent 2+ run margin estimate clearly exceeds the run line's implied probability. If you cannot make this estimate independently of the ML, cap the run line at 1 unit regardless of other factors."

WHY THIS ADDRESSES THE WEAKNESS: It severs the run-line evaluation from the ML evaluation and forces a separate probability estimate for the specific outcome the run line prices, which is exactly the link I broke on ATL -1.5 and LAD -1.5 when I cited the ML-to-run-line gap as primary evidence of edge.

SOURCE OF THIS INSIGHT: TOR @ ATL (best bet): I explicitly wrote "the ML has moved to approximately ATL -250 across books (implying ~71% win probability), but ATL -1.5 remains available at +103 (implying only ~49%) — a clear pricing gap" — but a team being 71% to win and 49% to win by 2+ are not contradictory prices; in a 7.5-total game with a dominant starter, many wins come by exactly 1 run.

EXPECTED IMPACT: HIGH
