## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When L10RS is the primary offensive strength signal for a team total projection, require cross-validation against available lineup-level wRC+ data before finalizing the run estimate. Specifically: if the confirmed or projected lineup avg wRC+ (vs opposing pitcher handedness) is more than 15 points below what L10RS implies, apply a downward adjustment of 0.5-0.8 runs to the team's projected offensive contribution.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** L10RS is a lagging indicator that reflects the last 10 games' scoring regardless of who was actually in the lineup. A team on a hot L10 streak may have featured a fully healthy, top-lineup configuration during that stretch, while today's game features rest days, platoon moves, or injury replacements that substantially dilute offensive quality. My current method uses L10RS as a team-level offensive signal without requiring lineup-level validation.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data, not outcome):** ATH L10RS was 7.0 — a strong signal used as the primary ATH offensive driver in my 14.5-run projection. The confirmed ATH lineup (avg wRC+ 86.0 vs RHP) included Colby Thomas (-32 wRC+), Jonah Heim (30 wRC+), and Henry Bolte (89 wRC+) — a meaningfully weaker offensive unit than 7.0 RS/G implied. This discrepancy was identifiable from lineup data: even without confirmation, a reasonable pre-game expectation of ATH's lineup construction should have flagged that their best offensive performers (Kurtz, Langeliers, Soderstrom) are not guaranteed to all appear in a given game's optimal configuration. The gap between L10RS signal and lineup-level signal was accessible in principle from the data structure I already receive.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — one game is insufficient to institutionalize a method change. This proposal should be evaluated over 15-20 similar cases where L10RS diverges from lineup wRC+ by >15 points before adoption. The directional logic is sound; the calibration threshold (15 points, 0.5-0.8 run adjustment) requires sample validation.
