## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When a team-level wRC+ vs pitcher handedness is flagged as extreme (≤60 or ≥130), require individual lineup spot review before finalizing the edge calculation — specifically identify whether the extreme average is driven by 2-3 outlier players or is broadly distributed. A broadly distributed extreme (6+ of 9 hitters below 70) is a stronger signal than a mean pulled by 2-3 very low/high individual scores with moderate names elsewhere.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** The CLE wRC+ 48 vs LHP was used as a monolithic team-level signal, but the confirmed lineup showed Manzardo (163), Hedges (139), and Schneemann (131) as legitimate threats within that aggregate. If only 3-4 hitters are truly suppressed while others are neutral or above average, the effective threat to the opposing starter is higher than the aggregate implies. The current method takes team-level aggregates at face value without stress-testing the distribution.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data):** CLE's team wRC+ 48 vs LHP was the primary basis for the MIL bet. Pre-game data did show individual splits were available in principle — the confirmed lineup revealed the distribution was not uniform. The original write-up cited "CLE's wRC+ vs LHP is a catastrophic 48" without qualifying whether this was broadly distributed or concentrated in a few very low hitters dragging down the mean.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — one-day evidence is insufficient to adopt, but the signal is directionally consistent: team aggregates that are extreme (in either direction) warrant a distribution check before using as a strong edge signal. Propose tracking this across 10+ slates to validate.
