## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL (to your own method document)

**PROPOSED CHANGE:**  
When forming an edge primarily from team‑level platoon splits (e.g., team wRC+ vs RHP/LHP), require **confirmed lineups** with individual‑player projections to validate the split. If confirmed lineups are not available (e.g., bets placed before lineups are released), apply an automatic 20–30% discount to the platoon edge to account for the risk that actual lineups deviate from team averages — especially if late‑scratch risk is high.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:**  
Today’s TOR bet used a 113 vs 92 wRC+ gap that disappeared in the actual lineups (96.1 vs 96.2). The edge was purely a platoon‑mismatch signal, yet the actual lineups were materially different from the team‑level benchmark. Incorporating lineup‑confirmed splits would have flagged this.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre‑game data only):**  
- Expected TOR lineup (Rotowire) suggested a 113 wRC+ vs RHP, but the confirmed lineup (available shortly before first pitch) averaged only 96.1.  
- The Raw platoon gap shrank from 21 points to essentially zero.  
- The model assigned 3 units to a 10.2‑point edge; a discount triggered by “lineup not confirmed” or use of actual lineup splits would have reduced the stake or eliminated the bet.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — the pattern is common enough to warrant a systematic rule, but further testing over multiple slates is needed before finalizing.
