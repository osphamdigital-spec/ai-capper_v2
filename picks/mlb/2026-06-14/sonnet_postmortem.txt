## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** Implement a "starter durability flag" that triggers a mandatory pass review when a starter's most recent outing was under 4.0 IP AND no L14 data is available. Separately, implement an "ERA regression alert" when a starter's ERA runs more than 1.0 run below their xFIP — treat this as a net-neutral or slight negative for the team the starter pitches for, not a positive.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** Currently, short recent outings (L3 IP data) are noted descriptively but not systematically converted into a decision rule. A starter with a sub-4.0 IP last start could be masking an injury or stuff deterioration that aggregated metrics cannot capture. This is particularly dangerous when combined with missing L14 data (as in Mikolas' case) because there is no medium-term baseline to anchor against. Similarly, the ERA-vs-xFIP gap is computed when analyzing individual starters but is not flagged as an explicit regression risk in the bet rationale, leading to cases (Elder) where a bet was partly built on a metric (ERA 2.66) that the process's own framework identifies as unsustainable.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data only):**
- Mikolas: L3 outings 3.1, 5.1, 4.0 IP + no L14 data → SEA bet lost on WAS scoring 10
- Harrison: L3 showing 2.1 IP / 8 ER + L14 flagged small sample → PHI bet lost on PHI scoring 0
- Elder: ERA 2.66 vs AGG xFIP 3.99 (gap 1.33) + L14 xFIP 4.09 → ATL bet lost on ATL scoring 1
- In all three cases, the pre-game data contained the specific signal; the method did not convert it into a clear decision rule

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — three data points in one day is not sufficient to adopt as a rule, but the pattern is consistent enough (all three losing bets involved this type of ignored pre-game signal) to warrant a proposal. Needs validation over 10+ additional slates before method adoption.
