## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL
PROPOSED CHANGE: For any bet graded 3 units, the starting pitcher must have (a) no small-sample flag, and (b) the team’s bullpen must be near-full (defined as no more than T3 empty, AND T1/T2 fully available). A bullpen noted as “slightly depleted” or “taxed” caps side bets at 1 unit regardless of edge size.
WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: The bullpen language is currently subjective. Today “slightly depleted but not chaotic” on LAD was treated as acceptable for 3u on a moderately established starter. That played out catastrophically. A hard rule prevents this drift.
EVIDENCE FROM TODAY: LAD bullpen flagged “slightly depleted”; Sheehan lacked a large-sample track record yet received 3u. Neither condition met the proposed 3u requirement. STL’s 3u bet, which had a “bullpens manageable” note and a more tested starter, would still pass.
CONFIDENCE: LOW — one-slate result is anecdotal, but the guardrail aligns with the pre-existing small-sample and bullpen-avoidance logic used elsewhere on the card.
