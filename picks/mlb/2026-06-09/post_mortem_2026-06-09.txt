SECTION 6 — MODEL-SPECIFIC REASONING INSTRUCTION

MODEL: Claude Opus 4.8

REASONING WEAKNESS IDENTIFIED:
I applied the liquid-market halfway-regression rule mechanically to a starter in visible, documented freefall (Gallen, last-14 xFIP 5.57 / L3 ERA 7.04), regressing a legitimate edge down to a no-bet lean — treating a public-but-acute pitcher decline as if it were generic market noise.

PROPOSED PROMPT INSTRUCTION:
"When applying the liquid-market regression rule, first check whether your edge rests on an acute, recent, and specific pitcher signal — a last-14 or last-3-starts collapse, a confirmed role change, or a documented form spike — rather than on season-long priors. If it does, state that signal explicitly and regress only one-quarter of the way toward the implied probability, not halfway. Full halfway regression applies only when your edge rests on stable, season-level inputs the market has had months to price."

WHY THIS ADDRESSES THE WEAKNESS:
The regression rule exists to curb overconfidence on stale priors, but a starter who has cratered over his last three starts is precisely the kind of recent, specific signal markets price slowly — so blanket halfway regression erases real edge.

SOURCE OF THIS INSIGHT:
The AZ @ MIA lean — I had Meyer over a collapsing Gallen at a 5-6 point raw edge and regressed it to a lean; the underlying read was correct and would have been a clean 1-unit bet.

EXPECTED IMPACT: MEDIUM
