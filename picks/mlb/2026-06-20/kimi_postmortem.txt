## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When a starting pitcher carries an **[sm] (small sample) flag** on L14 or seasonal aggregates, apply a Bayesian shrinkage penalty before feeding the number into the win-probability engine: regress the published AGG SIERA/xFIP **40% toward league average** (or the pitcher’s career baseline if available) rather than treating the raw AGG figure as a point estimate. If the [sm] flag is present, require a **minimum 5.0-point edge** (up from 3.5) to bet the side, and apply the same regressed figure to total run estimates.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** I currently treat AGG SIERA as a fixed true-talent estimate even when the model itself warns that the sample is small. This creates false precision and can inflate edges on volatile pitchers.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (cite pre-game data, not the outcome):** Will Warren entered with a 3.76 AGG SIERA but an **[sm] L14 flag**. I priced NYY at 69.1% (4.3-point edge) treating 3.76 as reliable. If regressed 40% toward a league-average ~4.40 SIERA, his effective run-suppression estimate becomes ~4.14, materially shrinking the gap vs. Abbott (5.07) and likely collapsing the 4.3-point edge toward market. The [sm] flag was the correct pre-game warning that the edge was overstated.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM
