## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

PROPOSED CHANGE: Require a stronger penalty for favorite bets when the projected offensive edge is modest and not yet lineup-confirmed; conversely, allow near-threshold underdogs to upgrade faster once confirmed lineups materially support the split thesis.  
WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: I was a little too comfortable converting a thin team-context edge into a favorite price on Washington without confirmation, while also staying too conservative on a near-even underdog like Miami despite lineup uncertainty that had more downside for the favorite than for the dog.

## Bottom-line self-review

What worked:
- Plus-money underdog selection in TOR was good process.
- Conservative passing on TBD-starter games was still correct process.
- Several leans were on the right side because the initial split and bullpen reads were directionally sound.

What failed:
- I overpriced Washington as a favorite without enough confirmed offensive separation.
- I was too slow to upgrade Miami and Arizona once lineup-sensitive logic would have supported it.
- The CHW lean shows I relied too much on broad team split language without enough concern for who was actually in the lineup.

What I’d change next slate:
- Be stricter laying juice before confirmed lineups.
- Be more aggressive upgrading near-threshold dogs/favorites once lineups clearly validate the split thesis.
- Treat “team crushes X-handed pitching” as provisional unless the actual posted lineup supports it.
