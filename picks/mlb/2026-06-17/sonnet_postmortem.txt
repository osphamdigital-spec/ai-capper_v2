## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When one side of a ML market is flagged as suspect/stale, use the opposing side's clean price as the market anchor rather than treating the entire ML market as absent, PROVIDED the edge direction from my analysis points to the suspect-priced side.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** The current rule ("price flagged stale/suspect → treat that market as absent") is overcautious when a clean price exists on the other side. If MIA ML is suspect but PHI ML is clean at -109, then PHI's implied probability (~52%) gives me a market anchor from which I can derive MIA's implied probability (~48%), and I can evaluate whether my estimated ~57% for MIA clears the 4-pt edge gate. The suspect-price flag was designed to prevent me from being exploited by stale pricing — not to prevent me from betting an edge that's derivable through the opposing side's clean price.

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data):** MIA @ PHI: MIA ML flagged suspect, PHI ML clean at -109. Pre-game process clearly identified MIA (Alcantara blended ~3.54 true ERA, PHI Painter declining with xERA 4.83 and worsening L3) as the correct side. Using PHI -109 as anchor: PHI implied ~52.1% → MIA implied ~47.9% → gap vs my ~57% estimate = ~9.1 pts → would have triggered a 1-unit MIA bet. The suspect-price flag was not designed for this scenario.

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM — one game is insufficient to adopt a rule change; this should be tracked across additional instances where the suspect-price flag blocks an otherwise well-supported edge to see if the win rate on such plays exceeds the no-flag baseline.
