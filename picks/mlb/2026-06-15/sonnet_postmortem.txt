## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** When both starters carry AGG xFIP anchors within 0.10 of each other (near-wash matchup), weight the *team offensive split vs. starter handedness* at the confirmed lineup level more heavily than the starter gap in determining bet structure (ML vs. RL) and edge calculation.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** In COL @ CHC, the starter gap (Imanaga 4.28 vs. Lorenzen 4.31 xFIP — essentially identical) meant the edge was almost entirely derived from COL's wRC+ 68 vs. LHP and CHC's wRC+ 104 vs. RHP. When the starter gap is near-zero, the offensive split becomes the primary signal, and the RL target (+100) may have been aggressive relative to the ML (-204) in a game where Lorenzen's ERA 7.54 introduced high variance regardless of process. The method should flag: *if starter gap < 0.15 xFIP points AND RL is the pick structure, require higher confirmed lineup offensive split confidence before committing to RL over ML.*

**EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (pre-game data only):** Imanaga AGG xFIP 4.28 vs. Lorenzen AGG xFIP 4.31 — 0.03 pt gap, essentially zero. The RL bet was almost entirely dependent on COL wRC+ 68 vs. LHP being sustained in-game. The [sm] flag on Imanaga L14 further reduced confidence in starter suppression. Pre-game, I noted the edge was "roughly a wash on process" for the starters — that language should have triggered a ML-only structure or a reduced unit size rather than RL.

**CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM** — one game is not enough to adopt this as a firm rule, but the pre-game data already contained the signal (near-identical xFIP anchors, L14 [sm] flag on home starter, RL dependent entirely on offense split) that should have pushed toward ML structure.
