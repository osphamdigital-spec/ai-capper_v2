## SECTION 6 — MODEL-SPECIFIC REASONING INSTRUCTION

MODEL: Claude (Opus 4.8)

REASONING WEAKNESS IDENTIFIED: I size stakes on the *number and richness of arguments* favouring a side ("a confluence of independent signals stack") rather than on the *magnitude of the probability gap* — so I ran my own price-vs-estimate check, correctly found only a 4-point edge, and still bet 3 units because the story felt strong. Both of my last two 3-unit best bets lost for exactly this reason.

PROPOSED PROMPT INSTRUCTION:
"Derive stake size ONLY from the numerical gap between your win-probability estimate and the price's implied probability — never from how many separate arguments support the side. State both numbers and the gap explicitly, then apply this single-game MLB mapping: gap under 5 points = maximum 1 unit; gap of 5–8 points = up to 3 units; gap above 8 points with clean, confirmed data = up to 5 units. A rich 'confluence of signals' that still nets only a small gap is a 1-unit play. If your gap calculation and your desired stake disagree, the gap wins."

WHY THIS ADDRESSES THE WEAKNESS: It severs stake size from narrative richness and binds it to the only quantity that determines expected value — the probability gap — which is precisely the link I broke on both PIT (-155, ~4-point gap, 3u) and the prior slate's CHW.

SOURCE OF THIS INSIGHT: PIT ML -155 at 3 units this slate, where I explicitly wrote "61% implied vs 65% estimate" and still staked 3 units — combined with the identical oversizing on CHW -121 last slate.

EXPECTED IMPACT: HIGH
