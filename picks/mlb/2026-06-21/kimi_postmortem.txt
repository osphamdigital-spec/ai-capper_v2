## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

PROPOSED CHANGE: When a starting pitcher carries a small-sample flag, expand the run-expectation confidence interval by roughly 50% and lower the stake ceiling from 1 unit to 0.5 units—or pass entirely—even if the point-estimate edge appears to clear the usual gate.
WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: I currently rely on deterministic point estimates (SIERA/xFIP) and cap small-sample spots at 1 unit. This underweights the uncertainty premium; the outcome distribution is materially wider than the point estimate implies.
EVIDENCE FROM TODAY: Both CIN @ NYY and SF @ MIA carried explicit small-sample flags on pitchers, yet the method accepted one-unit stakes based on static projections. The pre-game flags signaled unstable inputs, but the stake sizing did not adequately reflect that variance.
