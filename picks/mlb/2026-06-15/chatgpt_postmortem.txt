## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

PROPOSED CHANGE: Reduce confidence in team-level split edges for side bets unless they are supported by projected lineup-weighted hitting strength; where lineup data is missing, haircut offensive edges derived mainly from aggregate wRC+ splits.  
WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: I was too comfortable treating Arizona’s weak team wRC+ vs RHP as a stable offense-negative in the LAA cap. That kind of aggregate number can be distorted by injuries, bench exposure, and roster churn.  
EVIDENCE FROM TODAY (cite pre-game data, not the outcome): My LAA bet was built substantially on “Angels better offense split vs RHP” and “Arizona 80 wRC+ vs RHP.” In the lineup review, Arizona’s actual batting order was much closer to neutral overall and included several above-average bats vs RHP. On the other side, PIT’s confirmed lineup showed how much a lineup-weighted view can strengthen or weaken a team-level baseline.
