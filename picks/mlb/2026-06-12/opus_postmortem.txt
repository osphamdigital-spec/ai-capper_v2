PROPOSED CHANGE: When a starter carries an active L14 command-collapse flag (e.g., BB/9 spiking 3+ above baseline), apply a modest haircut to my edge estimate even when I'm betting the OTHER side's superiority — and a larger haircut when I'm relying on that collapsing arm's opponent's bullpen rather than its own hitting.
WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER: On ATL @ NYM, I noted McLean's L14 collapse but treated it as a reason to favor ATL. In a coinflip, a struggling-but-talented arm regressing toward his strong AGG line is real downside to my edge. I may be too quick to dismiss recent form as noise when the form signal is command-based (walks) rather than results-based (BABIP/HR luck). Command degradation predicts better than BABIP swings.
