## S4: METHOD CHANGE PROPOSAL

**PROPOSED CHANGE:** Implement a "star player availability flag" as a semi-required pre-game data point for any game featuring a top-15 MLB offensive player (by wRC+, OPS+, or fWAR) where that player has recent injury/maintenance risk. Specifically: if a player I cite by name in my pick reasoning (e.g., "Ohtani 172 wRC+") is absent from the confirmed lineup, the pick stake should automatically decrease by 50% (from 1u to 0.5u) or the gap estimate should be recalculated with that player removed before locking a stake.

**WHAT IN MY METHOD COULD BE SHARPER:** My current method uses team-level metrics (RS/G, wRC+ aggregate, rdiff) as primary offensive inputs. For most teams, this is appropriate — a 108 wRC+ lineup is roughly consistent regardless of which specific players are present on a given day. But for teams with one transcendent offensive player (Ohtani for LAD, Judge for NYY when healthy, etc.), the team-level metrics are heavily inflated by that one player. When that player rests or is injured, the team's actual offensive projection for that specific game can be 15-25% lower than the season aggregate suggests. My method does not currently have a systematic trigger to re-evaluate when a named key player is absent.
